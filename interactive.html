Interactive Mulitmedia

Throughout this semester, Ive focused on the interactiveness with Human and 3D objects in a 3D enviroment. I worked with programs such as Touch Designer, Unity, and VR. My work explores the blank space, a space that seems endless and allows the players to stay in the scene. Many of my works explore the ideas of nostalgia, memories, and play. This allowed for players to create their own ideas of my work, allowing for play and wonder. Enjoy all the work from my final semester of college.

Sketch 1

Software:

Touch Designer, Photoshop

Description:

Deer on the TV - This was my first assignment when it came to playing with interactiveness. The user is able to move the look of the tv, ecouraging displacement and illusion within the deer. They are slowed down to face eachother, and the user can control how to view the deer and where they run.

Sketch 2

Software:

Unity

Description:

Flying Objects - In this sketch, I worked with how objects react to movement and how they bounce. I added a bounce factor to all walls and objects which made them fly all over. As one of my first assignments in Unity, I wanted to play around with now objects react in a space with no gravity. All models were test prefabs from Unity Web Store.

Sketch 3

Software:

Blender, Unity

Description:

1st Claw Machine - This is the start of Sketch 4, as I wanted to build a claw machine that does not let objects leave it unless through the hole. This was a test on box colliders and raycasting with picking up the claw machine. I used test objects to test how glass would work and how to keep objects in a barrier without glitches.

Sketch 4

Software:

Blender, Unity, C#

Description:

Arcade - This sketch goes through a small arcade function that I made with a claw machine. This was my first time fully messing with C# outside of raycasting. The claw is functionable by arrow keys, with music playing as well. You cannot pick up the items with the claw, but you can pick them up yourself. This was my first project when messing with UI, and it taught me more on how unity works and how to create an interaction between the player and the game. Watch the video for more information on how it worked

Assignment 1: Artist Homage

Software:

Touch Designer, Procreate

Description:

Artist Homage - This work is based off of Stephen Tashjian, Tabboo!, and their work in the early 1990s. This is inspried off of their pyramid flyers and the line work. My work explores those lines in a manipulative way of displacement. The video shows how one user may displace the line. I also included my own artwork in the back that reminds the user of Tabboo!.

Assignment 2: IRL

Software:

Blender, Unity, C#, VR

Description:

Throw the TV - This is a small interactive VR world that plays old TV commericals on the TV everytime it comes in contact with the floor. The user is allowed to pick it up with remotes and throw it, fully being able to see 360 degrees around them. The videos change when it is touched to the ground, and shuffle through 3 different old videos. The play of the interaction is that the TV can disapear and the player is now left in the white room with no ends.
